# M1 — ERP Foundations

## Module Purpose

M1 introduces the student to ERP thinking.

The student acts as a Junior ERP Analyst assigned to understand a company before implementation begins.

The goal is not technical execution.

The goal is business understanding.

---

## M1-M01 — Discover the Company

**Business Objective:**  
Understand how the company currently operates.

**Business Context:**  
The company is preparing for its first ERP implementation. Departments use disconnected tools, information is fragmented, and management lacks visibility.

**Student Role:**  
Junior ERP Analyst.

**ERP Processes:**  
Business discovery, process observation, organizational analysis.

**Departments Involved:**  
Management, Sales, Purchasing, Inventory, Finance, Customer Service.

**ERP Modules:**  
ERP Core, Organization, Process Map.

**Mission Inputs:**  
Company profile, department list, product list, customer list, supplier list, current business issues.

**Student Tasks:**  
- Review the company profile.
- Identify main departments.
- Identify current business problems.
- Match problems to affected departments.

**Expected Decision:**  
Identify which departments are most affected by lack of ERP integration.

**Business Consequences:**  
The student understands why the company needs ERP integration.

**KPIs:**  
ERP readiness, department visibility, process fragmentation.

**Learning Outcomes:**  
The student can explain the company context and identify where information is disconnected.

**Assessment Criteria:**  
Correct identification of departments, business problems and affected areas.

**Estimated Duration:**  
25 minutes.

**Difficulty:**  
Beginner.

---

## M1-M02 — Connect Departments, Processes and ERP Modules

**Business Objective:**  
Understand how departments, processes and ERP modules connect.

**Business Context:**  
Management wants to understand how ERP will connect the business. The student must map business needs to ERP modules.

**Student Role:**  
Junior ERP Analyst.

**ERP Processes:**  
Process mapping, ERP module identification, business integration.

**Departments Involved:**  
Sales, Purchasing, Inventory, Finance, Customer Service, HR.

**ERP Modules:**  
Sales, Purchasing, Inventory, Finance, CRM, HR, BI.

**Mission Inputs:**  
Business needs, process examples, module cards, department cards.

**Student Tasks:**  
- Match business needs to departments.
- Match departments to business processes.
- Match processes to ERP modules.
- Identify cross-module dependencies.

**Expected Decision:**  
Select the correct ERP modules required to support each business situation.

**Business Consequences:**  
The student understands that ERP is an integrated business system, not a collection of isolated screens.

**KPIs:**  
Process coverage, module alignment, integration score.

**Learning Outcomes:**  
The student can connect business situations to departments, processes and ERP modules.

**Assessment Criteria:**  
Correct mapping between needs, departments, processes and modules.

**Estimated Duration:**  
30 minutes.

**Difficulty:**  
Beginner.

---

## M1-M03 — Diagnose ERP Readiness

**Business Objective:**  
Evaluate whether the company is ready to begin ERP implementation.

**Business Context:**  
Before implementation begins, the company must review data quality, process clarity, responsibilities and governance.

**Student Role:**  
Junior ERP Analyst.

**ERP Processes:**  
ERP readiness assessment, governance review, process preparation.

**Departments Involved:**  
Management, IT, Finance, Operations, Sales, Purchasing.

**ERP Modules:**  
ERP Core, Governance, Master Data, Process Map.

**Mission Inputs:**  
Readiness checklist, data issues, role descriptions, approval gaps, integration risks.

**Student Tasks:**  
- Review readiness indicators.
- Identify data gaps.
- Identify unclear responsibilities.
- Identify missing approvals.
- Classify risks by category.

**Expected Decision:**  
Determine whether the company is ready, partially ready or not ready for ERP implementation.

**Business Consequences:**  
The student understands that ERP success depends on preparation, not only software configuration.

**KPIs:**  
Readiness score, data quality score, governance risk level.

**Learning Outcomes:**  
The student can identify basic readiness gaps before ERP implementation.

**Assessment Criteria:**  
Correct classification of readiness issues and basic risk interpretation.

**Estimated Duration:**  
30 minutes.

**Difficulty:**  
Beginner.

# M2 — Master Data & Organizational Structure

## Module Purpose

M2 teaches that ERP processes depend on reliable structure and data.

The student prepares the company foundation before operational processes begin.

---

## M2-M01 — Structure the Organization

**Business Objective:**  
Create a clear organizational structure for ERP use.

**Business Context:**  
The company has departments, locations and responsibilities, but they are not clearly structured in the ERP environment.

**Student Role:**  
Junior ERP Analyst.

**ERP Processes:**  
Organizational setup, role mapping, structure validation.

**Departments Involved:**  
Management, Operations, Finance, Sales, Purchasing, Warehouse.

**ERP Modules:**  
Organization, HR, Finance, Inventory, ERP Core.

**Mission Inputs:**  
Department list, location list, warehouse list, role descriptions.

**Student Tasks:**  
- Review organizational units.
- Link departments to responsibilities.
- Identify locations and warehouses.
- Validate role ownership.

**Expected Decision:**  
Confirm the correct company structure for ERP foundation.

**Business Consequences:**  
The ERP environment becomes ready to support operational transactions.

**KPIs:**  
Structure completeness, role clarity, organizational readiness.

**Learning Outcomes:**  
The student can explain why company structure matters in ERP.

**Assessment Criteria:**  
Correct identification of departments, locations, roles and responsibilities.

**Estimated Duration:**  
30 minutes.

**Difficulty:**  
Beginner.

---

## M2-M02 — Create Essential Master Data

**Business Objective:**  
Prepare essential master data required for ERP processes.

**Business Context:**  
The company cannot run purchasing, sales or inventory processes until products, customers and suppliers are reliable.

**Student Role:**  
Junior ERP Analyst.

**ERP Processes:**  
Master data creation, master data validation.

**Departments Involved:**  
Sales, Purchasing, Inventory, Finance, Customer Service.

**ERP Modules:**  
Master Data, Sales, Purchasing, Inventory, Finance.

**Mission Inputs:**  
Product records, customer records, supplier records, units of measure, payment terms.

**Student Tasks:**  
- Review product records.
- Review customer records.
- Review supplier records.
- Validate required fields.
- Identify missing information.

**Expected Decision:**  
Approve or reject master data records for operational use.

**Business Consequences:**  
Reliable master data allows business processes to run correctly.

**KPIs:**  
Master data completeness, validation rate, missing field count.

**Learning Outcomes:**  
The student can identify essential master data and explain its role in ERP processes.

**Assessment Criteria:**  
Correct validation of product, customer and supplier data.

**Estimated Duration:**  
35 minutes.

**Difficulty:**  
Beginner.

---

## M2-M03 — Correct Data Quality Issues

**Business Objective:**  
Identify and correct common master data problems.

**Business Context:**  
The company has duplicate records, incomplete supplier files, incorrect customer information and inconsistent product categories.

**Student Role:**  
Junior ERP Analyst.

**ERP Processes:**  
Data quality review, data correction, master data governance.

**Departments Involved:**  
Sales, Purchasing, Inventory, Finance, Customer Service.

**ERP Modules:**  
Master Data, Governance, Sales, Purchasing, Inventory.

**Mission Inputs:**  
Duplicate customers, incomplete suppliers, incorrect product categories, missing payment terms.

**Student Tasks:**  
- Identify duplicate records.
- Identify missing required fields.
- Correct simple master data issues.
- Classify data risks.
- Validate corrected records.

**Expected Decision:**  
Determine which records are ready for use and which require correction.

**Business Consequences:**  
The student understands that poor data creates process errors downstream.

**KPIs:**  
Data quality score, duplicate rate, correction completion rate.

**Learning Outcomes:**  
The student can recognize and correct basic master data quality issues.

**Assessment Criteria:**  
Correct identification and correction of data quality problems.

**Estimated Duration:**  
35 minutes.

**Difficulty:**  
Beginner.

# M3 — Procure-to-Pay

## Module Purpose

M3 introduces the purchasing cycle from business need to supplier invoice preparation.

The student learns how procurement connects demand, suppliers, inventory and finance.

---

## M3-M01 — Identify a Purchasing Need

**Business Objective:**  
Recognize when the company needs to purchase goods.

**Business Context:**  
Stock is becoming insufficient for a product required by customers. The company must decide whether a purchase request is justified.

**Student Role:**  
Junior ERP Analyst.

**ERP Processes:**  
Demand review, purchase request, procurement planning.

**Departments Involved:**  
Inventory, Purchasing, Sales, Operations.

**ERP Modules:**  
Purchasing, Inventory, Sales, BI.

**Mission Inputs:**  
Stock levels, demand forecast, reorder threshold, product priority.

**Student Tasks:**  
- Review stock level.
- Compare demand with available quantity.
- Identify shortage risk.
- Create or recommend a purchase request.

**Expected Decision:**  
Decide whether a purchase request is required.

**Business Consequences:**  
The company can prevent shortage and protect customer service.

**KPIs:**  
Stock availability, shortage risk, purchase need score.

**Learning Outcomes:**  
The student can explain when purchasing should be triggered by business demand.

**Assessment Criteria:**  
Correct interpretation of stock and demand signals.

**Estimated Duration:**  
30 minutes.

**Difficulty:**  
Beginner to Intermediate.

---

## M3-M02 — Create and Process a Purchase Order

**Business Objective:**  
Transform a purchasing need into a supplier commitment.

**Business Context:**  
A purchase request has been approved. The student must select the appropriate supplier and prepare a purchase order.

**Student Role:**  
Junior ERP Analyst.

**ERP Processes:**  
Supplier selection, purchase order creation, approval.

**Departments Involved:**  
Purchasing, Finance, Inventory, Operations.

**ERP Modules:**  
Purchasing, Supplier Management, Finance, Inventory.

**Mission Inputs:**  
Approved purchase request, supplier list, prices, lead times, payment terms.

**Student Tasks:**  
- Review supplier options.
- Select supplier based on basic criteria.
- Create purchase order.
- Validate quantity, price and delivery date.
- Submit for approval.

**Expected Decision:**  
Choose the most appropriate supplier for the purchasing situation.

**Business Consequences:**  
The company creates a formal purchasing commitment and prepares future inventory receipt.

**KPIs:**  
Supplier reliability, purchase order accuracy, expected lead time.

**Learning Outcomes:**  
The student can explain how a purchase order formalizes procurement.

**Assessment Criteria:**  
Correct supplier choice, accurate order details and approval logic.

**Estimated Duration:**  
35 minutes.

**Difficulty:**  
Intermediate.

---

## M3-M03 — Receive Goods and Analyse Supplier Impact

**Business Objective:**  
Record receipt of purchased goods and analyse the business impact.

**Business Context:**  
The supplier delivers the ordered goods. The company must receive them, update inventory and prepare supplier invoice review.

**Student Role:**  
Junior ERP Analyst.

**ERP Processes:**  
Goods receipt, inventory update, supplier performance review, invoice preparation.

**Departments Involved:**  
Inventory, Purchasing, Finance, Operations.

**ERP Modules:**  
Inventory, Purchasing, Finance, Supplier Management.

**Mission Inputs:**  
Purchase order, delivery receipt, received quantity, expected quantity, supplier performance data.

**Student Tasks:**  
- Review purchase order.
- Compare expected and received quantities.
- Record goods receipt.
- Identify quantity differences.
- Review supplier impact.

**Expected Decision:**  
Accept receipt, flag discrepancy or request follow-up.

**Business Consequences:**  
Inventory is updated and finance can prepare supplier invoice processing.

**KPIs:**  
Receiving accuracy, supplier delivery performance, inventory update status.

**Learning Outcomes:**  
The student can explain how goods receipt connects purchasing, inventory and finance.

**Assessment Criteria:**  
Correct receipt logic, discrepancy identification and supplier impact analysis.

**Estimated Duration:**  
35 minutes.

**Difficulty:**  
Intermediate.
