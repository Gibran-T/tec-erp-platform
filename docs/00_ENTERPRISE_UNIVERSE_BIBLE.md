# 1. Constitutional Preamble

## 1.1 What This Document Is

This is not a course outline.  
This is not a software specification.  
This is not a simulator design.

This is the **Enterprise Universe** of TEC.ERP — a complete, living Canadian company that students do not study but **join**.

Students do not attend a class. They accept a job.  
They receive an offer letter, an employee number, a manager, a mentor, a probation period, and a place in a company that remembers every decision they make.

Software exists in this universe only because companies exist.  
ERP exists only because business processes exist.  
Business processes exist only because people collaborate.  
People collaborate because companies pursue value.

The student must understand this without ever reading a definition.  
They must **live it**.

## 1.2 Absolute Pedagogical Principle

**Never teach software first. Teach life inside a company.**

The ERP is not the subject of the experience.  
The company is the subject.  
The ERP is simply where work happens — the same way Outlook, Teams, and Excel are where work happens, without anyone calling them "the course."

## 1.3 One Company, One Story, One Memory

Every student enters the same company: **NordHabitat Équipements Inc.**

Every mission, email, KPI shift, supplier delay, customer complaint, board decision, and promotion path occurs inside this single continuity.

The company evolves. The student evolves. The relationship evolves.

Actions have consequences. The company remembers.

## 2.2 Origin Story

NordHabitat was born in a Saint-Laurent warehouse in 1987 when Rémy Beauchemin, a mechanical engineer trained at Polytechnique Montréal, recognized that Québec's commercial construction boom needed more than equipment — it needed **intelligent building infrastructure** that could be sourced, assembled, supported, and maintained locally.

What began as a regional HVAC components distributor became, over four decades:

- A **national distribution network** with six Canadian distribution centres
- A **light assembly and configuration facility** in Laval for building automation kits
- A **technical services division** supporting contractors, property managers, and institutional clients
- A **TSX-listed mid-cap** respected by analysts for operational discipline and ESG transparency

The company did not become large by accident. It became large by mastering the unglamorous middle: **getting the right component to the right building at the right time, with documentation, compliance, and support.**

That mastery is now under strain — and that strain is where the student arrives.

# 3. Corporate Identity & Culture

## 3.1 Logo Philosophy

The NordHabitat mark is a **stylized roofline merged with a circuit trace** — representing the union of physical building infrastructure and digital intelligence.

- **Primary colour:** NordHabitat Blue `#005A7A` (trust, engineering precision)
- **Secondary:** Copper Accent `#C47A2A` (warmth, energy, Québec craftsmanship)
- **Neutral:** Slate `#2D3436`, Cloud `#F4F6F8`

The logo never appears without bilingual tagline in official communications:
*« L'intelligence du bâtiment » / "Building Intelligence"*

## 3.2 Internal Communication Style

- **Default:** Professional, warm, direct
- **French/English:** Match the recipient's last-used language in Teams/email; internal announcements are bilingual by policy
- **Tone:** Never corporate-empty. Names are used. Recognition is specific.
- **Acronyms:** Defined on first use in any external-facing or cross-department document
- **Signature block:** Standardized — name, title, department, extension, pronouns optional

## 3.3 Meeting Culture

| Meeting type | Cadence | Duration | Culture |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily stand-up (operational teams) | Daily | 15 min | Standing (virtual or physical), blockers only |
| Department weekly | Weekly | 45 min | KPI review + escalations |
| Cross-functional sync | Bi-weekly | 60 min | Process issues, handoffs, ERP impacts |
| Monthly business review | Monthly | 90 min | Data-driven, no slide decks over 12 pages |
| Quarterly town hall | Quarterly | 60 min | CEO + executive team, Q&A encouraged |
| Board meeting | Quarterly | Half day | Formal, pre-read mandatory 72h ahead |

**Meeting rules:** Agenda required. Notes published within 24h. Decisions tagged with owner and date.

## 3.4 Decision-Making Philosophy

**"Inform broadly. Decide narrowly. Document always."**

- Operational decisions → closest accountable manager
- Cross-department process decisions → process owner + affected VPs
- Financial commitments → delegated authority matrix (see §6.3)
- Strategic bets → Executive Committee + Board approval

Disagreement is expected. Passive resistance is not.

## 3.5 Approval Hierarchy (Summary)

| Commitment type | Approval authority |
|---|---|
| Purchase request < $5,000 CAD | Department manager |
| Purchase order $5,000 – $25,000 | Director + Procurement |
| Purchase order $25,000 – $100,000 | VP + Finance review |
| Purchase order > $100,000 | CFO or delegate + Executive sign-off |
| New supplier onboarding | Procurement + Compliance + Finance |
| Customer credit limit change | Finance + Sales director |
| Master data creation (critical) | Data steward + department owner |
| ERP configuration change (production) | ERP CoE + IT Change Advisory Board |

## 3.7 Innovation Culture

Innovation is **pragmatic, not theatrical**. NordHabitat does not have a foosball-and-buzzwords culture. It has:

- **Le Lab NordHabitat** — small innovation cell reporting to CTO
- **Annual Innovation Challenge** — employees propose process improvements; winners implemented
- **Pilot tolerance** — small experiments encouraged if reversible and measured
- **ERP CoE** — bridge between business innovation and system reality

## 3.8 Sustainability Strategy

- **2027 target:** 35% reduction in Scope 1+2 vs. 2019 baseline
- **Green packaging programme** in all DCs
- **Supplier ESG scorecard** mandatory for top 50 suppliers
- **Electric fleet transition** for Montréal last-mile delivery (pilot in student year)
- **Student exposure:** Sustainability KPIs appear in procurement and logistics scenarios

## 3.9 Quality Policy

**"Zero compromise on what enters a building."**

- ISO 9001:2015 certified (Laval Assembly & Configuration Centre)
- Incoming inspection protocols at all DCs
- RMA root-cause analysis mandatory for repeat failures
- Quality holds ERP status that blocks shipment

## 3.10 Cybersecurity Policy

- MFA mandatory on all corporate accounts
- No shadow IT without exception approval
- Phishing simulations quarterly
- Data classification: Public / Internal / Confidential / Restricted
- Incident severity P1–P4 with defined escalation (see IT section)
- Student completes security awareness in Week 1

## 3.11 AI Strategy

**"Augment people. Never bypass accountability."**

- AI Transformation Office (ATO) established 2024
- Digital personas for key roles (see §15)
- AI recommends; humans approve
- No autonomous financial transactions
- All AI interactions logged for audit
- Student experiences AI as Claire, Marc, Julie — never as "the AI"

## 22.1 Claire Fontaine — Senior Business Analyst, ERP Center of Excellence

**THE MENTOR**

### Complete Biography

Claire Élise Fontaine was born in 1983 in Rosemont–La Petite-Patrie, Montréal, the middle of three sisters. Her father, Gilles Fontaine, was a métro maintenance supervisor for the STM; her mother, Louise, taught French literature at CEGEP de Maisonneuve. Claire grew up in a household where precision met poetry — her father measured tolerances in millimetres while her mother debated the meaning of a single word in Racine. Claire learned early that **the right word and the right number can both prevent disasters.**

She did not plan a corporate career. After her BA in History at Université de Montréal (2005), she took a six-month temp assignment at NordHabitat in 2009 to "pay rent while figuring out grad school." She was assigned to document warehouse receiving procedures. She spent three days on the DC floor with receivers who had worked there longer than she had been alive. She came back with forty pages of notes and a question no one had asked: *Why does the system say 40 when the shelf says 36?*

Rémy Beauchemin, still CEO then, heard about the temp with the clipboard. He asked to meet her. The meeting lasted twelve minutes. He said: *« Tu poses les bonnes questions. Reste. »* She stayed.

Fourteen years later, Claire is the institutional memory of NordHabitat's transformation — not because she knows ERP, but because she **knows the building**. Every process she maps, she maps by walking to the person who lives it.
