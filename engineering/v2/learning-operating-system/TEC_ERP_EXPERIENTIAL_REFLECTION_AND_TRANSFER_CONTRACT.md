## Official cycle

1. Experience  
2. Observe  
3. Reflect  
4. Extract principle  
5. Reapply  
6. Compare  
7. Consolidate  

## Required elements of every major simulation

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Learner observation | What changed in process/Twin |
| Decision rationale | Why the choice was made |
| Unexpected outcome | What surprised |
| Extracted principle | Portable rule |
| Alternative context | Another company/situation |
| Short reapplication task | Principle tried again briefly |

## Required reflection prompts (FR)

- Qu’avez-vous observé?  
- Quelle hypothèse avez-vous utilisée?  
- Quelle preuve était décisive?  
- Quel résultat vous a surpris?  
- Quel compromis avez-vous accepté?  
- Quel principe retenez-vous?  
- Que feriez-vous dans une autre entreprise?

EN equivalents MAY be provided; FR prompts remain canonical for bilingual parity planning.

## Transfer standard

Transfer is proven when the learner:

1. States a principle not tied only to one screen.  
2. Applies it in a different context or chain link.  
3. Compares the new outcome to the original.  
4. Consolidates into professional language (recommandation / risque / contrôle).

## Reflection MUST NOT become

- Decorative journaling  
- Generic emotional feedback  
- Character-count exercises  
- AI-generated text accepted without learner synthesis  

## AI boundary

Visible AI may help structure reflection questions. The learner’s synthesis remains the assessable artifact. AI text alone is not evidence of transfer.
