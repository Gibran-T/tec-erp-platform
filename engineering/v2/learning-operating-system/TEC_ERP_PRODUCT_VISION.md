# TEC.ERP Product Vision

> **Status:** AUTHORITATIVE PRODUCT NORTH STAR · CONTRACT ONLY — NOT IMPLEMENTED  
> **Authority:** TEC.ERP Learning Operating System Master Contract · Owner review required before Wave 2.  
> **Role of this document:** Product manifesto · decision compass · onboarding identity · design filter · pedagogical filter · technical-governance reference.  
> Read this before every detailed contract.

---

## 1. One-Sentence Vision

**TEC.ERP is a professional learning simulation in which learners enter a persistent living enterprise, investigate incomplete situations, act under control, observe consequences, and form evidence-based judgment as ERP analysts and consultants.**

## 2. What TEC.ERP Is

TEC.ERP is a **Learning Operating System**.

It is not a course shelf attached to software, and not software with a few lessons bolted on. It is one coherent product that combines:

- an academic portal;  
- a professional ERP simulator;  
- a persistent **Digital Twin**;  
- a digital classroom;  
- a process laboratory;  
- **BI Studio**;  
- an **AI Decision Workspace**;  
- an **Enterprise Life Engine**;  
- a **Teaching Deck**;  
- Professor orchestration;  
- competency evidence;  
- assessments;  
- a separate **Capstone**;  
- evidence-based certification.

In TEC.ERP, the learner does not study isolated screens. The learner enters a company, investigates it, acts within it, sees consequences, communicates with stakeholders, and develops professional judgment. Modules, missions, documents, KPIs, and AI are instruments of that judgment — not destinations in themselves.

Academic structure remains visible — Module, learning objective, competency, assessment, certification — so institutions can teach and certify with integrity. Professional narrative remains dominant in Simulation — Mandat, Mission, Dossier, Comité, Recommandation — so learners rehearse the language of practice.

The product exists to form people who can understand, transform, and advise a living enterprise.

## 3. What TEC.ERP Is Not

Clarity begins with refusal. TEC.ERP is not:

- a generic LMS;  
- a list of modules;  
- a launcher;  
- an ERP clone;  
- a collection of forms;  
- a text-heavy manual;  
- a dashboard collection;  
- a points-based game;  
- a chatbot added to missions;  
- a replacement for the Professor;  
- a collection of disconnected scenarios;  
- a system where completion equals competence.

If a proposed design makes TEC.ERP behave like any of the above, it fails the vision — even if it is technically elegant.

## 4. The Professional We Intend to Form

TEC.ERP is designed to form ERP analysts and consultants who can operate with process discipline, evidence, and executive clarity.

The learner evolves from:

**observer → junior analyst → process analyst → ERP consultant → trusted advisor.**

Along that path, the product expects the learner to:

- understand end-to-end processes;  
- diagnose business problems;  
- work with master and transactional data;  
- execute controlled ERP actions;  
- interpret documents;  
- analyze KPIs;  
- understand operational and financial consequences;  
- communicate with stakeholders;  
- recognize risk and governance issues;  
- use AI responsibly;  
- justify decisions with evidence;  
- formulate executive recommendations;  
- connect business, people, process, data, and technology.

TEC.ERP does not exist to form button-clickers. A learner who can navigate every menu but cannot explain why a purchase order, a reception variance, or a cash timing shift matters has not yet met the product’s purpose.

## 5. The Learner Experience We Want

The learner should feel oriented, professionally responsible, challenged but not lost, supported but not carried. They should feel inside a living enterprise, aware that decisions matter, able to see progress, able to connect modules, able to explain why an action matters, able to make and defend decisions, and increasingly autonomous.

Within five seconds of entering a surface, the learner must understand:

- where they are;  
- who they are (professional role);  
- what **mandat** they have;  
- what state the company is in;  
- what they must do next.

Guidance fades with mastery. Novices receive segmentation, worked examples, and clear roles. Advanced learners receive ambiguity, competing priorities, delayed consequences, and the duty to defend a recommendation. The product never confuses denser text with deeper learning.

Progress should feel dual: the company evolves, and the learner’s professional identity evolves with it. Seeing both journeys at once is part of the experience we want — not a hidden curriculum.

## 6. The Professor Experience We Want

The Professor is facilitator, mentor, orchestrator, observer, challenger, debriefer, evaluator, and final pedagogical authority.

TEC.ERP must amplify the Professor. It must not reduce teaching to unlocking content or checking scores. The platform prepares the room, stages the enterprise, surfaces anonymized evidence, and holds the Digital Twin coherent — while the Professor teaches.

The Professor should be able to prepare, present, demonstrate, pause, trigger events, observe, compare, question, correct, debrief, intervene, assess, validate progression, review Capstone, and issue certification.

A classroom that runs without Professor judgment is incomplete. A product that replaces Professor judgment is out of vision.

The Teaching Deck and Presentation Mode exist so the Professor can teach from the platform itself — with process maps, ERP demonstration anchors, anonymized evidence, KPI stories, and debrief questions — without exporting the course into a parallel slide world that drifts from the Twin.

## 7. The Living Enterprise

At the center of TEC.ERP sits one persistent fictional enterprise — the learning universe that continues across the journey.

It contains people, departments, customers, suppliers, products, warehouses, accounts, cost centers, orders, purchases, receipts, invoices, payments, service cases, HCM records, risks, controls, KPIs, and audit events. The same enterprise evolves as the learner progresses. Modules do not invent private contradictory worlds; they open different doors into the same company.

Every important learner action should produce at least one visible reaction: a data change, a document change, a KPI change, a stakeholder response, a risk, an approval, an objection, an escalation, or a delayed consequence. Silent important actions are prohibited.

Metaphor for the product identity:

**The Digital Twin is the body of the enterprise.  
The Enterprise Life Engine is the water that circulates through it and gives it voice, movement, and reaction.**

The body holds facts. The water carries messages, pressure, and response. Neither may invent a different company than the one the Twin authorizes.

## 8. The Role of AI

TEC.ERP uses two forms of AI, and they must remain distinguishable.

**Visible AI** supports learner reasoning. It explains, asks questions, identifies gaps, helps compare alternatives, and supports reflection. It lives primarily in the AI Decision Workspace and related coaching surfaces.

**Ambient AI** gives voice to stakeholders, animates communication, creates governed reactions, and simulates organizational life through the Enterprise Life Engine.

AI must never:

- replace learner judgment;  
- fabricate evidence;  
- mutate authoritative state by itself;  
- decide for the learner;  
- expose answer keys;  
- approve Capstone;  
- issue certification;  
- contradict the Digital Twin;  
- replace Professor authority.

The learner remains the author of the final conclusion. AI may prepare the mind; it may not sign the recommandation.

## 9. How Learning Happens

Learning in TEC.ERP is decision-first and consequence-aware.

It combines authentic **mandats**, incomplete information, investigation, guided practice, simulation, decision, consequence, reflection, debrief, and reapplication. The learner experiences four official modes:

- **Exploration** — safe discovery without irreversible official mutation;  
- **Guided Practice** — scaffolded steps with explanatory feedback;  
- **Simulation** — authentic responsibility with Twin consequences;  
- **Evaluation** — restricted assistance and immutable evidence.

Every module follows the official cycle:

**Découvrir → Apprendre → Observer → Pratiquer → Analyser → Réfléchir avec l’IA → Valider → Débriefer.**

Theory may support judgment; it must not open the door. Mandats begin with a business problem, available and missing evidence, constraints, urgency, and a decision point — not with a wall of text.

## 10. The Three-Mission Logic

Each module contains exactly three regular missions with distinct pedagogical roles.

**Mission 1 — Enquête et diagnostic**  
Investigate, frame, form a hypothesis, map evidence.

**Mission 2 — Intervention ERP et contrôle**  
Execute controlled ERP action and produce transactional proof.

**Mission 3 — Conseil, récupération et décision**  
Interpret consequences, recover where needed, and advise with executive clarity.

Progression in one line:

**understand → act → advise**

The three missions must never be three similar tasks. Similarity without pedagogical distinction violates the product.

## 11. What Makes TEC.ERP Unique

TEC.ERP is unique not because of one feature, but because these elements work together as one coherent learning system:

- a persistent Digital Twin;  
- connected end-to-end processes;  
- consulting mandats;  
- visible consequences;  
- BI as decision practice;  
- AI as coach and company-life engine;  
- Professor-centered teaching;  
- evidence-based competency;  
- historical run integrity;  
- a separate Capstone;  
- a versioned curriculum;  
- an integrated Teaching Deck;  
- continuous professional identity.

Remove any one of these and the product becomes thinner. Replace them with a launcher, a game score, or a chatbot, and the product ceases to be TEC.ERP.

## 12. Non-Negotiable Principles

These principles may never be broken.

1. No module exists without a business purpose.  
2. No mandate starts with a wall of theory.  
3. No mission exists without a professional role.  
4. No important action disappears silently.  
5. No consequence is reduced to a score.  
6. No KPI moves without a traceable cause.  
7. No AI response overrides evidence.  
8. No AI replaces learner judgment.  
9. No Professor is reduced to an administrator.  
10. No historical run is rewritten.  
11. No V1 history is disguised as V2.  
12. No Capstone is treated as a normal module.  
13. No BI experience is only text.  
14. No analytical answer is constrained to a tiny field.  
15. No screen exists without a clear next step.  
16. No image exists without a teaching purpose.  
17. No simulation ends without debrief.  
18. No reflection ends without reapplication.  
19. No advanced learner remains permanently guided.  
20. No novice receives uncontrolled complexity.  
21. No production wave is approved by report alone.  
22. No major implementation begins without a real Playback.  
23. No feature is accepted because it merely works technically.  
24. No interface may look like the first TEC.ERP with more words.  
25. No product decision may weaken the identity of a living professional learning simulation.

## 13. Product Quality Bar

TEC.ERP must meet or exceed TEC.WMS in portal, login, and classroom clarity. It must exceed TEC.WMS in enterprise integration, BI, AI, evidence, and Professor control.

It must feel modern, visual, coherent, and professional. It must avoid generic SaaS appearance and long-form document behavior. It must use progressive disclosure. It must support Light, Dark, and Projector modes. It must support FR and EN. It must remain accessible, teachable in a real classroom, understandable to a new learner, and auditable with historical trustworthiness.

A feature that works in a demo but fails in a classroom fails the quality bar.

## 14. Decision Filter

Before approving any feature, ask:

1. Does it help the learner understand the enterprise?  
2. Does it support professional judgment?  
3. Does it create or use meaningful evidence?  
4. Does it make consequences visible?  
5. Does it support the Professor?  
6. Does it preserve historical integrity?  
7. Does it reduce or add unnecessary cognitive load?  
8. Does it strengthen the Digital Twin?  
9. Does it respect AI governance?  
10. Does it look and feel like the future TEC.ERP?

A feature that fails these questions must be revised or rejected — even if it ships cleanly, passes unit tests, or impresses in isolation.

Use this document as the filter that sits above every contract: *Does this still feel like TEC.ERP?*

## 15. Closing Vision

TEC.ERP is not designed to teach learners how to navigate software.

It is designed to form professionals capable of understanding, transforming, and advising a living enterprise.

When screens, AI, KPIs, and missions forget that purpose, they must be brought back. When they serve that purpose together — with the Professor at the center, evidence as the currency of competence, and the Digital Twin as the shared world — TEC.ERP becomes what it was always meant to be: a Learning Operating System for the next generation of ERP analysts and consultants.
