## Purpose

Every consulting **mandat** is a decision case: the learner enters as a professional facing an incomplete situation, not as a reader of theory.

## Mandatory mandate opening package

Every consulting mandate MUST begin with:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Enterprise context | Company, unit, process chain location |
| Protagonist or sponsor | Named sponsor / rôle professionnel |
| Business problem | Concrete problem statement |
| Available evidence | What exists now (docs, KPIs, messages) |
| Missing information | Explicit gaps |
| Constraints | Policy, budget, capacity, compliance |
| Urgency | Time pressure and deadline |
| Competing priorities | At least one tension when pedagogically relevant |
| Decision point | What must be decided |
| No revealed answer | Answer keys hidden from learner |

**Hard rule:** No mandate may begin with a wall of theory.

## Required learner sequence

1. Receive the mandate.  
2. Form an initial hypothesis.  
3. Investigate.  
4. Identify evidence.  
5. Compare alternatives.  
6. Decide.  
7. Execute where appropriate.  
8. Observe consequences.  
9. Defend the decision.  
10. Reconsider after debrief.

Modes map roughly: Exploration supports steps 1–4 discovery; Guided Practice scaffolds 2–7; Simulation requires full 1–10; Evaluation assesses 3–9 under restricted assistance.

## Professor facilitation questions

Use during class or checkpoints:

- What do you know?  
- What do you assume?  
- What evidence is missing?  
- Who is affected?  
- What alternatives exist?  
- What risk are you accepting?  
- What would make you change your decision?  
- What would you tell the executive sponsor?

## Deliverable expectations

| Phase | Typical artifacts |
|-------|-------------------|
| Investigation | Hypothesis, evidence map, anomaly list |
| Decision | Alternatives compared, chosen option, risks accepted |
| Defense | Synthèse exécutive for sponsor / comité |
| After debrief | Revised principle + short reapplication |

## Anti-patterns

- Starting with glossary or chapter text  
- Single forced “happy path” disguised as a case  
- Revealing the recommended answer in the briefing  
- Skipping defense or debrief  
- Accepting AI text as the learner’s decision defense
