## Purpose

Learners develop a situated professional identity as ERP / process consultants inside one coherent enterprise, while academic structure remains visible for assessment and certification.

## Professional progression

| Stage | Identity | Expectation |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Observer | Orient, name objects, follow process without irreversible writes |
| 2 | Junior analyst | Investigate, document evidence, apply controls with guidance |
| 3 | Process analyst | Diagnose cross-step issues; propose controlled interventions |
| 4 | ERP consultant | Execute and defend decisions under competing priorities |
| 5 | Trusted advisor | Executive synthesis, trade-offs, governance-aware recommendations |

Dual journey pairing: see `TEC_ERP_DUAL_JOURNEY_ROADMAP_CONTRACT.md`.

## Professional language (prefer)

mandat · dossier · preuve · diagnostic · hypothèse · anomalie · contrôle · arbitrage · recommandation · comité · synthèse exécutive · impact · risque · décision

## Avoid (when professional language is clearer)

activity · generic exercise · answer box · next page · click to complete

## Professional practices to develop

| Practice | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| Stakeholder communication | Role-appropriate messages with authority awareness |
| Evidence-based writing | Claims tied to documents/KPIs/Twin facts |
| Executive communication | Short synthèse for sponsor / comité |
| Professional notes | Structured dossier notes, not chatty diary |
| Escalation | When and how to raise risk |
| Decision defense | Alternatives, risks accepted, evidence |
| Cross-functional collaboration | Impact across departments and chains |

## Dual vocabulary

| Academic (visible) | Professional narrative |
