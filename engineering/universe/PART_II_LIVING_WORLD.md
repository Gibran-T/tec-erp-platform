## 22.1 Claire Fontaine — Senior Business Analyst, ERP Center of Excellence

**THE MENTOR**

### Complete Biography

Claire Élise Fontaine was born in 1983 in Rosemont–La Petite-Patrie, Montréal, the middle of three sisters. Her father, Gilles Fontaine, was a métro maintenance supervisor for the STM; her mother, Louise, taught French literature at CEGEP de Maisonneuve. Claire grew up in a household where precision met poetry — her father measured tolerances in millimetres while her mother debated the meaning of a single word in Racine. Claire learned early that **the right word and the right number can both prevent disasters.**

She did not plan a corporate career. After her BA in History at Université de Montréal (2005), she took a six-month temp assignment at NordHabitat in 2009 to "pay rent while figuring out grad school." She was assigned to document warehouse receiving procedures. She spent three days on the DC floor with receivers who had worked there longer than she had been alive. She came back with forty pages of notes and a question no one had asked: *Why does the system say 40 when the shelf says 36?*

Rémy Beauchemin, still CEO then, heard about the temp with the clipboard. He asked to meet her. The meeting lasted twelve minutes. He said: *« Tu poses les bonnes questions. Reste. »* She stayed.

Fourteen years later, Claire is the institutional memory of NordHabitat's transformation — not because she knows ERP, but because she **knows the building**. Every process she maps, she maps by walking to the person who lives it.

### Career History

| Year | Role | Defining moment |
|---|---|---|
| 2009 | Temp admin, Operations documentation | Discovered DC-MTL inventory discrepancy pattern |
| 2010 | Operations Coordinator | Implemented first cross-DC receiving checklist |
| 2012 | Business Analyst, Process Improvement | Led value stream map for Laval assembly line |
| 2015 | Senior Business Analyst | Survived failed CRM migration; learned "technology without adoption is theatre" |
| 2018 | Founding member, ERP Center of Excellence | Named by Sylvie Roy; charter author |
| 2020 | Lead BA, Projet Équinoxe | Wrote first 47 process requirements alone in a weekend during COVID |
| 2023 | Senior BA + unofficial mentor programme | Trained Lucas Bergeron; formalized junior analyst onboarding |
| 2025 | Mentor to Employee #NHE-XXXX | Her culminating mentorship — by design, her last |

### Education

- BA History, Université de Montréal (2005)
- Certificat en analyse d'affaires, HEC Montréal (2013)
- PMP certification (2016, lapsed 2021 — she says she "manages humans, not Gantt charts")
- Internal NordHabitat Academy: Supply Chain Fundamentals, Finance for Non-Finance (2014–2018)

### Professional Evolution

Claire evolved from **documenter** → **questioner** → **bridge-builder** → **mentor**. She never became a manager by choice. She refused twice. Her leadership philosophy: *"If I manage people, I stop being available to them."* Sylvie Roy accepted this — Claire's title stayed Senior BA, but her influence exceeds many directors.

### Family Situation (Pedagogically Relevant)

Claire lives alone in a condo in Villeray with a rescue cat named Voltaire. Her father passed in 2022; she wears his maple pin on her lapel at work — not for patriotism alone, but because *"he built things that had to work every day, like the métro."* She visits her mother Sundays for dinner. This matters pedagogically: Claire understands **work-life integration** without preaching it. When she tells the student to go home at 17:30 on a normal day, she means it. When she stays until 21:00 during go-live week, she also means that.

### Leadership Philosophy

*"My job is to make you unnecessary to me."*

- Questions before answers
- Ownership before blame
- Process before tool
- People before deadline — except when buildings depend on delivery

### Communication Style

Warm, precise, Socratic. Uses silence as a tool. Never raises voice. When disappointed, becomes quieter, not louder. Bilingual instinctively — French with Montréal warmth, English with McGill clarity.

## 22.2 Isabelle Marchand — President & Chief Executive Officer

### Complete Biography

Isabelle Marchand was born in 1971 in Outremont, Montréal, to a cardiologist father and a nurse administrator mother. She grew up watching **systems save lives** — not software systems, human ones: triage protocols, shift handoffs, supply chains for blood and oxygen. She learned that **delay kills** and **clarity saves**.

She studied Mechanical Engineering at McGill (1993), then worked two years at Bombardier before realizing she preferred **how organizations move** to how metal moves. MBA at HEC Montréal (1998). Joined NordHabitat in 2001 as Sales Director for institutional accounts — hospitals, universities, municipal buildings. She sold the Hôpital du Sacré-Cœur their first major retrofit package in 2004. Dr. Catherine Meunier remembers: *"Isabelle didn't sell equipment. She sold reliability."*

COO from 2012. CEO from 2019 when Rémy Beauchemin retired. First woman CEO in company history. First CEO not from engineering — **from the customer side**. This defines her: she thinks from the building outward.

# 32. Phase 11 — The Living Company Laws

## 32.1 Constitutional Laws (Inviolable)

1. **People always come before systems.**  
2. **ERP exists to support people — not the reverse.**  
3. **Every decision has consequences** — simulated and remembered.  
4. **Every communication has purpose** — no filler emails.  
5. **Every scenario advances the company timeline** — no side quests.  
6. **Every employee has memory** — NPCs reference shared history.  
7. **Every department has objectives** — not decorative org chart.  
8. **Every business process exists because a customer exists.**  
9. **Students are employees** — never "learners" in-universe.  
10. **Learning emerges naturally through work** — no lecture mode.  
11. **Reality has priority over complexity** — floor before screen.  
12. **Human interaction has priority over technology.**  
13. **Technology amplifies people — never replaces human judgment.**  
14. **The student must feel employed.**  
15. **The company must feel alive.**

## 32.2 Claire Laws (Mentor Inviolables)

- Claire never completes student transactions
- Claire never chooses for the student
- Claire never disappears without narrative cover
- Claire's final words are always: *"You don't need me anymore."*

## 32.3 Memory Laws

- No state reset mid-year without explicit replay mode
- Consequence chains must propagate
- Trust scores affect dialogue tone

## 32.4 Voice Laws

- Bilingual authenticity — not Google Translate
- No generic corporate empty phrases
- Every named character speaks distinctly

## 32.5 World Laws

- One company: NordHabitat Équipements Inc.
- One timeline: 30 hours = 1 year
- One geography: Canadian operations primary
- One ERP project: Projet Équinoxe

## 32.6 Implementation Laws (For Future Builders)

When building software from this bible:

- If a screen has no business narrative, delete the screen
- If a notification has no sender with a face, add a sender
- If a mission has no emotional stake, rewrite the mission
- If an AI persona approves money, remove the capability
- If the student is called "user" or "learner" in-universe, fix immediately
